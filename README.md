# Working Capital Optimization Through Supply Chain Finance
### A Multi-Sector Analysis of Indian Listed Companies and TReDS Adoption

## Overview

This repository contains my Master's Thesis submitted as part of the MBA program at ABV-IIITM Gwalior.

The project investigates how Supply Chain Finance (SCF) can optimize working capital management across Indian industries. It combines financial statement analysis, Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC) benchmarking, MSME survey analysis, and evaluation of India's Trade Receivables Discounting System (TReDS) ecosystem.

The research aims to identify sector-wise working capital challenges and evaluate how digital Supply Chain Finance solutions can improve liquidity, reduce financing costs, and enhance MSME participation.

---

## Research Objectives

- Analyze working capital efficiency of Indian listed companies using Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC).
- Compare working capital performance across multiple industries.
- Assess Supply Chain Finance awareness among Indian MSMEs.
- Evaluate TReDS adoption and barriers.
- Study the role of public and private sector banks in promoting SCF solutions.
- Recommend strategies for improving SCF adoption in India.

---

## Methodology

### Secondary Research

- 50 Listed Indian Companies
- 5 Financial Years (FY2021–FY2025)
- 5 Major Industries
    - FMCG
    - Automotive
    - Energy
    - Metals
    - Pharmaceuticals

Financial metrics analyzed:

- Working Capital
- Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)
- Days Payables Outstanding (DPO)
- Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC)

---

### Primary Research

- 44 MSME respondents
- Structured Questionnaire
- Multiple Indian States
- Supply Chain Finance awareness analysis
- TReDS adoption analysis
- Bank outreach effectiveness

---

## Key Findings

- Pharmaceutical companies recorded the highest average Cash Conversion Cycle, indicating significant working capital pressure.
- Automotive companies demonstrated negative CCC, reflecting highly efficient working capital management.
- Less than 15% of surveyed MSMEs actively used Supply Chain Finance solutions.
- More than 65% of respondents reported that their banks had never informed them about SCF or TReDS.
- Despite low awareness, a majority of MSMEs expressed willingness to adopt SCF if properly educated.
